About Me

I am a dedicated professional currently serving as an Assistant Professor in the Department of Electrical Engineering at the Government Engineering College, Khagaria, located in Raun, Alauli, Bihar, India. With over 5 years of academic experience, I am passionate about teaching, research, and guiding the next generation of engineers.

I completed M.Tech in Control and Instrumentation from Dr. B. R. Ambedkar National Institute of Technology Jalandhar, Punjab, India. I am currently pursuing a Ph.D. (part-time) in the Department of Instrumentation and Control Engineering at the National Institute of Technology Tiruchirappalli, Tamil Nadu, India. I believe in creating an engaging learning environment that combines theoretical knowledge with practical applications.

My professional journey has encompassed diverse areas within Power System and Control Engineering, reflecting his versatile skill set and broad expertise. My areas of interest span a broad spectrum of topics, including control theory and applications, circuit design, model order reduction, optimization, robotics, renewable energy, and control of lower extremity exoskeletons. My multidisciplinary approach to research underscores his innovative thinking and willingness to explore new avenues for technological advancement. Throughout my career, I have been actively involved in various research projects funded by esteemed organizations such as the Government of India, the Council of Scientific and Industrial Research (CSIR), and the Science and Engineering Research Board (SERB).

I have have published over 13 research articles in reputable national and international journals and conferences. In addition to my research contributions, I serve as an editor for the Journal of Control Science and Engineering (CSE) published by the Science Publishing Group.

UG Projects Guided

Solar based electric vehicle charging circuit in three phase G2V and V2G modes of operation

4 Students 2025

The project focuses on developing a solar-powered electric vehicle (EV) charging circuit capable of operating in both Grid-to-Vehicle (G2V) and Vehicle-to-Grid (V2G) modes using a three-phase system. It ensures efficient bidirectional power flow, enabling sustainable energy utilization and grid support.

Modeling & Simulation of Three Phase Grid Connected Solar PV System

3 Students 2025

This project involves the modeling and simulation of a three-phase grid-connected solar PV system to analyze its performance under varying solar and grid conditions. It aims to ensure efficient power injection into the grid while maintaining stability and power quality.

Load frequency control of two area power system using PID controller

3 Students 2024 Research Paper Accepted for Publication in E2ACON NITJ 2025

This project focuses on load frequency control of a two-area interconnected power system using a PID controller to maintain system frequency and tie-line power within acceptable limits. The PID controller ensures stable operation by minimizing frequency deviations due to load disturbances.

Intelligent Cloud Integrated Enhanced Irrigation System

3 Students 2024 Best Paper Award at E2ACON NITJ 2025

This project presents an intelligent irrigation system integrated with cloud computing to enable real-time monitoring and automated water control based on soil and environmental conditions. It enhances water efficiency and crop yield by leveraging IoT sensors and data-driven decision-making.

Vehicle Accident Detection and Alert System

5 Students 2023

This project develops a vehicle accident detection and alert system using sensors and microcontrollers to identify collisions in real time. Upon detection, it automatically sends the location and alert message to emergency contacts or services, enabling faster response and assistance.
